Environmental management

  • Agri Home
  • Environmental management

India's approach to environmental management is multifaceted, encompassing a range of legislative frameworks, governmental bodies, and policy initiatives aimed at protecting its diverse natural resources and mitigating environmental degradation. Key legislation such as the Environment (Protection) Act of 1986, the Forest (Conservation) Act of 1980, the Wildlife (Protection) Act of 1972, and various pollution control acts form the backbone of this management system. The Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) at the central level, along with State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs) and other agencies, are responsible for implementing and enforcing these regulations.

Despite the established framework, India faces significant environmental challenges stemming from rapid industrialization, urbanization, and a large population. These challenges include severe air and water pollution, deforestation, biodiversity loss, and increasing volumes of waste. The effectiveness of environmental management is often hampered by issues such as inadequate enforcement, limited resources, and the complexity of coordinating across different levels of governance. Balancing economic development with environmental sustainability remains a critical challenge for the country.

This includes promoting renewable energy, implementing stricter pollution control measures, and emphasizing waste management and the circular economy. Efforts are also being made to enhance climate change adaptation and mitigation strategies. Furthermore, there's a growing emphasis on public awareness and participation, along with the integration of environmental considerations into broader development planning to ensure a more sustainable future for the nation.
